# Example Research Run: "AI Email API Market in 2025"

## Input

**Topic:** What is the current state of AI email APIs in 2025? Who are the major players, what features do they offer, and what gaps exist in the market?

**Config:**
- Max rounds: 3
- Research agents: 4
- Depth: Standard

## Pipeline Execution

### Phase 1: Deep Research (4 agents)

**Agent 1 (Primary):** Gathered data on major email API providers:
- SendGrid (Twilio): Transactional + marketing, 100B+ emails/month
- Mailgun (Sinch): Developer-focused, strong API
- Resend: Modern DX, React Email integration
- Amazon SES: Low-cost, high-volume
- Postmark: Transactional-focused, high deliverability
- Customer.io: Behavioral automation
- Market size: $12.3B (2024), growing 13.2% CAGR

**Agent 2 (Critical):** Found issues:
- Most providers lack native AI/ML features
- Deliverability claims are often unverifiable
- Pricing complexity hides true costs
- Developer experience varies wildly
- Lock-in is common due to proprietary templates

**Agent 3 (Comparative):** Competitive landscape:
- Gap between transactional (Resend, Postmark) and marketing (Customer.io, HubSpot)
- No provider bridges both well at developer-friendly price points
- New entrants (Resend, Loops) focus on DX over features

**Agent 4 (Emerging):** Trends:
- AI-powered content generation is emerging
- Real-time personalization gaining traction
- Email-to-AI pipelines (inbound parsing + LLM)
- Compliance requirements increasing (GDPR, CCPA)

### Phase 2: Profile Building

Research Profile compiled with:
- 12 key findings
- 8 data points
- 4 contradictions identified
- 3 knowledge gaps
- 15 sources cited

### Phase 3: Round 1 — Adversarial Interrogation

**Team A (Validators):**
- Market size data: Strong (multiple sources agree)
- Provider feature lists: Strong (verified from official docs)
- Gap analysis: Moderate (based on inference, not survey data)
- Trend predictions: Weak (emerging, unproven)

**Team B (Challengers):**
- Found: Missing enterprise perspective (focused on developers)
- Found: No quantitative user satisfaction data
- Found: Pricing analysis was surface-level
- Found: Geographic bias (US-centric)
- **5 improvement points identified**

### Phase 4: Synthesis & Round 2

Improvements merged:
- Added enterprise buyer perspective
- Found G2/Capterra ratings for quantitative comparison
- Deep-dived into pricing tiers
- Added EU and APAC market data

**Round 2 Team A:** Confidence improved from 62% to 78%
**Round 2 Team B:** 2 improvement points remaining (minor gaps)

### Phase 5: Round 3 & Final Report

**Round 3 Team B:** No significant flaws found. Research validated!

## Final Report (excerpt)

### Executive Summary
The AI email API market in 2025 is a $12.3B industry growing at 13.2% CAGR, dominated by SendGrid and Mailgun but being disrupted by developer-first entrants like Resend. The key market gap exists between affordable transactional APIs ($0-20/mo) and enterprise automation platforms ($200+/mo) — no provider effectively bridges this gap with modern developer experience and native AI capabilities.

### Key Findings
1. **Market is fragmented by use case** — Strong evidence ★★★★☆
2. **Developer experience is the new battleground** — Strong evidence ★★★★☆
3. **AI integration is nascent but accelerating** — Moderate evidence ★★★☆☆
4. **Pricing opacity is a market-wide problem** — Strong evidence ★★★★☆
5. **The mid-market gap is real and exploitable** — Moderate evidence ★★★☆☆

### Confidence: HIGH (78%)
Research survived 3 rounds of adversarial interrogation. Remaining uncertainties: exact market size for AI-specific email features, long-term competitive response from incumbents.
